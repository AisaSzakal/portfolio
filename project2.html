The Ordinary Minds had the mission of creating a website that can help university students.
Our vision was to help students solve the daily challenges students face, like providing solutions for simple and affordable recipes, fostering friendships, identifying suitable communities, and managing a budget, all within a single platform. To make it functional we had to carried out a lot of research, but in the end, if we could lightening the load on students' shoulders, I believe the effort was truly worthwhile.

My part:
Aside the designs, ideas, research and planning part that we worked on together, I mainly focused on the social page for the website.

Target group:
In this group project, we did not have any specific information or data about our project itself. To address this extensive research became our initial focus, to learn more about the needs and interests of the end user. We conducted several interviews, surveys, and tests (mainly with students), to identify key areas of importance and what aspects to prioritize and what to leave behind. After the pizzeria project, I had gained some insights into conducting interviews, but in this project I still could improve the skill of fast thinking. We engaged in the task of synthesizing the gathered data, pinpointing the primary challenges faced by students—centered around cooking, socializing in a new environment, and managing a budget. This phase proved crucial in shaping our developmental process to align with the genuine needs of the end users.

User study report



Iterative process and interactive prototypes:
After working with the research, and summaries, we we transitioned from research to envisioning our website's design and functionality. Collaborating with fellow students, we tested prototypes (over and over again) to refine the product based on user needs. We started with a paper prototype, which we advanced to an interactive Figma version about the layout, and just after that, we started with the colors, fonts, and design elements. In this phase, we continuously integrated feedback into our process. This long process ensured our design, to implement it nicely in HTML and CSS. SO in the end our website not only met user needs but also received aesthetic validation. The experience emphasized how considering everyone's needs and focusing on user-friendly design is crucial in making digital products that work well for people (students) interacting with machines.

Targeted interaction:
In this project, we had to think just like a student, since our target was university students. While relating to the audience came naturally, we sought to enhance our understanding through various research tests. During this phase one of the key factors was the communication, since we aimed to make the target group understand what our goal are, but on the other hand to have benefical tests we should not manipulate the participants to say what we wanted to hear. During the first group project, a designated leader managed the group dynamics, allowing me to focus primarily on my individual tasks. However, the dynamic shifted in the second group project, where everyone had to be mindful not only of their own responsibilities but also of others' contributions. This experience emphasized the diversity within our team—some members thrived with more guidance, while others preferred working independently. Recognizing and adapting to these varied working styles became essential so to have a great communication betweeen us, and with the teachers. All in all, I've gained valuable insights into understanding people's approaches to work, and I am confident that I can now quickly assess and adapt to different working styles in the future, contributing to more effective collaboration.
